Kylespeak Hitlist

In July of 2018, I set out to stem the tide of personal jargon in my practice. The list represents terms and concepts that have become fixed shorthands in my writing and speech, and the plain alternatives that should replace them.

The List

accessories

libraries, tools, and other software used to build other software

commons club

a community that gives away work that only other members can use

convenience

work you do that helps others save time

defaults

legal rules that apply if you don’t say anything to change them

fulcrum

a source of leverage, to force others to consider making a deal with you

hack-and-slash

an edit of a document that makes strident edits, often deletions of entire sections or schedules

contrast surgery

network power

the ability to set rules in communities that you host

overrides

ways to change the rules that apply if you don’t say anything

private license

a license to one specific user

reciprocity in coin

rules that require others to pay you

contrast reciprocity in kind

reciprocity in kind

rules that require others to give back code

contrast reciprocity in coin

surgery

an edit of a document that makes minimal, target edits

contrast hack-and-slash

tuning

choosing a balance between what you give away and what you require back

withholding

choosing what not to give away for free
